STRENGTH TRAINING

Strength training, also known as resistance training or weight training, is a form of exercise that focuses on improving muscular strength and endurance.
It involves using resistance to induce muscular contraction, leading to the development of strength, size, and tone in skeletal muscles.
Strength training can be performed using various methods and equipment, including free weights (dumbbells, barbells), resistance machines, resistance bands, and bodyweight exercises.

Advantages of Strength training


  • Muscular Strength and Endurance:Strength training helps increase the force that muscles can exert.
  • Bone Health:Regular strength training has been shown to increase bone density, which is important for overall bone health and can help prevent osteoporosis.
  • Metabolism:Building and maintaining muscle mass can contribute to an increase in basal metabolic rate (BMR).
  • Fat Loss:Strength training, when combined with a balanced diet, can contribute to fat loss. Muscles require energy, and the more lean muscle mass you have, the more calories your body burns, even at rest.
  • Mental Health:Exercise, including strength training, has been linked to improved mood and reduced symptoms of depression and anxiety.
  • Disease Prevention: Strength training may help prevent or manage chronic conditions such as heart disease, diabetes, and arthritis.

CARDIO EXERCISES

Cardiovascular exercise, often referred to as cardio, is any exercise that raises your heart rate and enhances the efficiency of your cardiovascular system.
These activities promote the circulation of oxygen through the blood to the body's tissues and help improve overall cardiovascular health.

Advantages of Cardiovascular Exercise


  • Cardiovascular Health:Cardio exercise strengthens the heart, allowing it to pump blood more efficiently.
    It helps lower blood pressure by promoting better blood vessel function and reducing overall systemic resistance.
  • Weight Management:Cardiovascular exercise is effective for burning calories, which can contribute to weight loss or weight maintenance when combined with a balanced diet.
  • Improved Lung Function:Regular cardio activity enhances lung capacity and improves respiratory function, allowing the body to take in and use oxygen more efficiently.
  • Increased Energy Levels:Engaging in regular cardio exercise can boost energy levels by improving circulation and oxygen delivery to tissues.
  • Disease Prevention:Cardio exercise plays a role in preventing various chronic conditions, including cardiovascular diseases, type 2 diabetes, and some types of cancer.
